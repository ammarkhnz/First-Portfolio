Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam (1931–2015) Dr. Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam was an Indian aerospace scientist, author, and the 11th President of India (2002–2007). Known as the “Missile Man of India”, he played a major role in developing India's missile and nuclear programs. Born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, he came from a humble background and rose to become one of India's most respected and loved figures. He worked with ISRO and DRDO, where he led projects like the Agni and Prithvi missiles. As President, he was known for being people’s president, especially inspiring the youth of India with his speeches and books like “Wings of Fire” and “Ignited Minds”. Even after his presidency, he remained active in education and youth development. He passed away on July 27, 2015, while delivering a lecture at IIM Shillong — doing what he loved most: teaching and inspiring.
